Cocktail Facts

Fact 1

The word "cocktail" is believed to have originated from various sources, including the practice of using a rooster's tail (or "cock tail") as a garnish, or the French word "coquetel" (an eggcup used as a drinking vessel).

Fact 2

During Prohibition in the United States (1920-1933), speakeasies and underground bars flourished, leading to the invention of many classic cocktails that are still popular today.

Fact 3

The Martini, one of the most iconic cocktails, has evolved significantly over the years. The original recipe called for gin, sweet vermouth, orange bitters, and a lemon twist, while modern versions typically use dry vermouth and may include vodka instead of gin.
